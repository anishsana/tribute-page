Bill Gates

The main who put a computer in every home


Bill Gates at the 2012 World Economic Forum

Here's a timeline of Gates' life:

  • 1955: William Henry Gates III is born to attorney William Henry Gates II and Mary Gates, a schoolteacher, University of Washington regent and chairwoman of United Way International.
  • 1967: Gates is enrolled in the Lakeside School, an exclusive private school in Seattle, where he befriends Paul Allen.
  • 1973: Gates enters Harvard as a pre-law major, but soon shifts focus.
  • 1975: From his dorm room, Gates calls MITS, the maker of the world’s first personal computer
  • 1976: Gates and Allen register the trademark “Microsoft.
  • 1977: Gates takes a second leave of absence from Harvard and sets up Microsoft in Albuquerque, N.M., where MITS is headquartered.
  • 1978: Microsoft’s year-end sales exceed $1 million.
  • 1979: Microsoft headquarters are relocated to Bellevue, Wash., a suburb of Gates’ hometown, Seattle.
  • 1981: Microsoft incorporates and buys the rights to the operating system “DOS” from Seattle Computer Products
  • 1983: Allen leaves Microsoft after developing Hodgkin’s disease.
  • 1986: Microsoft headquarters move again, this time to nearby Redmond, Wash.
  • 1987: At age 31, Gates becomes the youngest billionaire ever
  • 1989: Gates founds Corbis, an archive of art and photography from public and private collections.
  • 1990: The Federal Trade Commission begins an investigation into possible collusion between IBM and Microsoft.
  • 1994: Gates weds French; the couple goes on to have three children.
  • 1995: In July, at age 39 and with a fortune of $12.9 billion, Gates becomes the world’s richest man.
  • 1998: In Belgium, on his way to meet business and government leaders, Gates is hit in the face with a cream pie
  • 1999: Gates publishes another best-selling book, Business @ the Speed of Thought, about the interconnectedness of business and technology.
  • 2000: Gates steps down as CEO of Microsoft. Gates’ former Harvard dorm-mate and right-hand man Steve Ballmer takes over the helm, while Gates becomes chief software architect.
  • 2001: Judge Penfield’s 2000 decision is overturned on appeal
  • 2005: Queen Elizabeth II bestows an honorary knighthood on Gates for his contributions to the United Kingdom.
  • 2006: Gates announces that his role as an executive at Microsoft will be phased out over the following two years.
  • 2007: Gates “graduates” from Harvard; the university awards him with an honorary degree.
  • 2008: Though his fortune continues to grow, Gates loses his spot as the richest man on the planet.

"The first rule of any technology used in a business is that automation applied to an efficient operation will magnify the efficiency. The second is that automation applied to an inefficient operation will magnify the inefficiency." - Bill Gates
